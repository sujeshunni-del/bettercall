Part 1: Schengen vs. Non-Schengen

Understanding the difference is vital. A Schengen visa grants access to 29 countries, but not all of Europe.

The Schengen Area is a zone of 29 European countries that have abolished internal borders.

The 29 Countries: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria*, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania*, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, and Switzerland.

*Bulgaria and Romania joined in March 2024 (air/sea borders only).

Part 2: Visa Types

Type C Short-Stay (90 Days)

Tourists, business trips, or family visits. Strict 90/180 day rolling limit.

Type D National Long-Stay

For living and working (>90 days). The primary visa for employment and residency.

Crucial for avoiding rejection. Do not use "Lump Sum" deposits.

Tip: Officers look for consistent salary credits or business income over 6 months, not just the final balance.

Part 7: Police Clearance (PCC)

What is it? Official criminal record summary.

Requirement: Mandatory for almost all Type D Work Visas.

Must be issued within the last 3-6 months and often requires Apostille.

Part 8: Apostille & Verification

Apostille

Stamp verifying authenticity for Hague Convention countries. Replaces legalization.

Superlegalization

For Non-Hague countries (e.g. UAE to Canada). Requires MFA + Embassy stamps.
